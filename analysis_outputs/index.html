Stakeholder takeaways
  • Two stories, not one. About four in five organizations (486 of 619) concentrate between 3× and 10× Medicare — a broad, structural pattern of pricing above the government reference, with dispute win rates in those bands typically around 80–83%.
  • A narrow tail drives the scary average. Only 47 organizations (~8%) sit at 50× Medicare or higher; that small group is what inflates the 67× simple average. Oversight can prioritize the tail without assuming every one of the 619 looks like the average.
  • The KPI count is not a “winners only” list. The headline 619 does not require that an organization win most disputes. If you add a strict “won more than lost” screen, the list shrinks to 572 — still large, but a different policy choice.
  • Sample size caveat. About 78% of these organizations have fewer than ten qualifying lines in the period; treat per‑organization averages as directional there. 135 have ten or more lines and support firmer comparisons.
  • Air ambulance is separate. Comparable work on ambulance benchmarks yields 87 organizations — almost all different from this 619 — and should be reported on its own slide if leadership cares about that line of business.
