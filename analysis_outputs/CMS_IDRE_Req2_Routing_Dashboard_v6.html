Statistical Confirmation — Win Rate Gaps Are Not Random

Two-Proportion Z-Test

A z-score measures how statistically unlikely a win rate gap is by chance. Z > 3.0 means p < 0.003; every flagged provider exceeds this threshold. z = 31.76 (NPPA SERVICES) would occur by random chance less than 1 in 1,000,000 times.

Key observation: Every flagged provider exceeds z = 3.0. NPPA SERVICES at z = 31.76 would occur by random chance fewer than 1 in 1,000,000 times. This is not a data anomaly — it is a behavioral pattern confirmed by statistical law.

Case-Mix Complexity Control — Routing Advantage Survives the Strongest Defense

RVU-Matched Case-Mix Analysis

The most plausible innocent explanation for a win rate gap is that providers route harder cases to a favorable IDRE because they are genuinely harder. This step tests that claim using Medicare RVU weights as a complexity proxy. If providers have similar or easier cases at their preferred IDRE, the defense fails.

Case Complexity Ratio vs. Win Rate Gap — RVU-Matched

X = RVU ratio (primary / others). X < 1 = simpler cases at primary; X > 1 = harder cases. Y = win rate gap (pp). Size ∝ line items at primary IDRE.
● Defense FAILS (similar complexity)  ● Defense BACKFIRES (simpler cases)  ● Partial defense (harder cases)
Provider ↕ Gap (pp) ↕ RVU Ratio ⓘ Verdict
The RVU ratio (x-axis) measures case complexity at a provider's preferred IDRE relative to all other IDREs, using Medicare Relative Value Units as the proxy. A ratio below 1.0 means cases at the preferred IDRE are simpler; above 1.0 means harder. If the "harder cases" defense were valid, flagged providers should cluster to the right. The y-axis shows how large their win rate gap is. Key observation: 12 of 13 flagged providers cannot use "harder cases" as a defense — their case complexity at the preferred IDRE is the same or lower than at other IDREs. Two providers (EMCC STEPHENVILLE, ER24 DENTON) route their simplest cases to Capitol Bridge and still win 47–51 pp more there. The routing advantage cannot be explained by clinical complexity — it is structural.
